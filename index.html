Travel

The race course changed in 2017 so it is also quite new. So the organizers decided to go for a route that would go through the old town, the most current center and the most futuristic part of the city. In addition to that, many of the slopes of the initial route were eliminated. The test begins in front of the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building and goes through some of the most iconic places in the Japanese capital.
For example, those who are lucky enough to run it will pass through the Imperial Palace, through the Tokyo Tower, through the largest fish market around the world and will end up at Tokyo Station - Gyoko-dori.

Better times

Since 2017 the times are much faster thanks to the change of route that we have commented. Now we are facing a much flatter and more attractive terrain for the runner.
For example, in 2010, Salim Kipsang finished the marathon in 2:12:19, while this year it finished in 2:04:15.

Men

The record for the Tokyo marathon is at 2:03:58 for men. It was achieved by Kenyan runner Wilson Kipsang in 2017.
The second best time is 2:04:15. It was achieved by the Ethiopian Birhanu Legese in 2020.

Women

The best time in the women's category is held by the Israeli Lonah Chemtai Salpeter, with a time of 2:17:45. He got it in the 2020 edition.
The second fastest time is in the possession of the Kenyan Sarah Chepchirchir, who in 2017 finished the race in 2:19:47.
